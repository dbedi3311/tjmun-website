JCC Catalan Secession: Spanish Cabinet

Single Delegation

Background Guide

Topic 1: Managing the Parliament of Catalonia

Topic 2: Dealing with Carles Puigdemont

Chair: Anshu Sharma

   Hello, delegates, and welcome to the Spanish Cabinet at TechMUN 2018! I am a member of the TJHSST Class of 2017 and the Randolph-Macon College Class of 2021. This is my first time chairing a committee, and I cannot tell you how honored I am to be serving in this position. Ever since my freshman year at TJ, serving at TechMUN has been a high point of my school year, and I’m sure this year’s TechMUN will be a high point in my MUN career.
   One of the immediate benefits of MUN is that it forces us to consider the complexities of modern day from others’ perspectives-including those from our own history. I hope you’ll compare and contrast the views of your character to those of others, and enter-and leave-this committee with lots of questions. If you have any for committee staff right now, please email spanishtechmun2018@gmail.com. Enjoy the spring, keep reading the news, and get excited. We are going to have a great time!

Director: Niharika Vattikonda

Delegates,
   Welcome to TechMUN 2018! My name is Niharika Vattikonda, and I’m so excited to be serving as your crisis director for JCC: Catalan Secession. I look forward to seeing both committees take decisive action on both of our topics for each side of the JCC. I’m a senior at TJ, and I’ve been part of TJMUN since my freshman year, and this is my 6th year of Model UN. Outside of MUN, I serve as Student Representative to the Fairfax County School Board, as Director of Internal Affairs for Coding Lady Colonials, and an organizer for HackTJ. In my free time, you’ll probably find me rewatching Friends or reading political op-eds.
   For those of you who are new to crisis, unlike your chairs, I will not be serving on the dais, but rather I will be creating crises for committee to solve and responding to your directives behind the scenes. In both public and private directives, I will be looking for creative solutions that approach the issues at hand in unconventional ways and draw from the lessons of previous actions to propose more effective solutions in this committee. For individual delegates’ private directives, I hope to see well thought-out arcs that adapt to the changing crisis environment and ultimately promote your own interests in committee. Don’t hesitate to reach out to us at cataloniatechmun2018@gmail.com. Good luck in your research and I look forward to seeing you in April!

Chair: Jeremy Krill

Delegates,
   My name is Jeremy Krill, and I’m your co-chair for the Spanish Cabinet. I’m a junior at TJ, and have been participating in MUN since middle school. Instead of doing homework like many other juniors my age, I like to use my time in a productive manner, which includes sleeping, playing Fortnite, and admining various meme accounts on a number of social media platforms. Seriously though, once I’ve finished studying for physics and calculus, I use my remaining 5 minutes of the day to play club and school soccer, launch model rockets, run track, manage the extensive funds of our school’s Catholic Student Association, and do more physics. You can also catch me whipping around for fun, listening to music (basically anyone but Russ), and watching Black Mirror.
   For this committee, you’ll be faced with frequent challenges with which you’ll have to respond quickly and efficiently. In other words, you’ll have to be thinking on your feet. Although the many nuances of the Catalan Secession Movement will undoubtedly leave you with uncertainty, the possibilities for responses are endless. I can’t wait to see your creativity unleashed in this committee, and I’m sure I’ll be surprised by each and every one of you! Have fun with your preparation- I can’t wait to see y’all in April!
