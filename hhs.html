US Health and Human Services, 2018

Single Delegation

Background Guide

Topic 1: The Opioid Crisis

Topic 2: Reforming Medicaid

Chair: Varun Saraswathula

Hello delegates!
   My name is Varun Saraswathula and I’m one of your co-chairs this year for the Department of Health and Human Services. As a senior at TJ, this is my fourth TechMUN on staff, but I’ve been coming since middle school. When I’m not munning, I love to read, run, watch TV (especially the Office and New Girl), and listen to music. Recently, I’ve picked up guitar, and practicing is one of my favorite ways to pass the time!
   I look forward to meeting all of you in April, and seeing what you do in HHS! With the conclusion of the first year of the Trump presidency, there’s a lot to discuss, and I’m excited to hear your ideas about how our nation can move forward. If you have any questions at all, please reach out via email at hhstechmun2018@gmail.com, and we’ll respond as soon as we can!

Chair: Ishaan Dey

   Welcome to TechMUN 2018! My name is Ishaan Dey, and I’ll be your co-chair for the Department of Health and Human Services. I’m a senior at TJ, and this is my 6th TechMUN (two as a middle school delegate, and this will be my fourth as a staffer). A little about me: I’m a portrait photographer, dance choreographer, and recently, found myself as an advocate for school mental health policy.
   I am very excited for this committee. Over the past year or so, there have been many developments in healthcare policy, but most of them have not received much attention. As it is, healthcare is very complicated, but under the Trump Administration, it has been changing very fast. Because of that, there is absolutely no substitute to solid research, and we’ll expect you to come in fully aware of healthcare policy as it relates to your position. I envisioned this committee be something that brings together multiple stakeholders under the current administration’s objections, but also to force perspective into how the current officials at the state, federal, and industry level are operating. Please reach out to me or Varun if you have any questions (healthcare is hard, we expect questions); you can find us at hhstechmun2018@gmail.com. We look forward to seeing you in April!
